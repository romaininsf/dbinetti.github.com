About

I'm one of the early pioneers of the Lean Startup™ movement and successfully have applied the methodology in my own startups as well as for clients.

My last company, Votizen, leads a chapter in Eric Ries's #1 Bestseller "The Lean Startup." I was honored to include Steve Blank, Sean Parker, Ashton Kutcher, Peter Thiel, and Ron Conway among my investors and was thrilled to lead Votizen to a successful acquisition in 2013.

I've also been on the founding team of five other startups, including QFN.com, which became Quicken.com (Intuit), Arch Rock (acquired by Cisco), and USA.gov (named USA Today's Site of the Year in 2000.)

I received my Bachelors with Highest Honors and General Distinction from UC Berkeley, and my MBA at UCLA where my team received the Deloitte Consulting Award for Top Thesis in the graduating class. I live in San Carlos, CA, with my wife and two daughters.

I'd love to help you and your orgainzation reach your objectives.

Services

I provide a range of services to companies seeking to employ the Lean Startup™ methodology to their organizations.

  • Consulting
  • Coaching
  • Workshops
  • Training

Consulting

I provide a full range of consulting services from business modeling and product development to go-to-market planning and metric assessment. I work closely with your executive team and managers to ensure alignment to your business goals.

Coaching

I work 1:1 with your executive team and key stakeholders on effective tactics to bring agile marketing and development practices to your company. Gain insights into the advantages of the Lean approach and the likely challenges you'll face in implementation.

Workshops

I hold one- to three-day workshops that introduce the Lean Startup concepts and provide a framework for your company to get started with the methodology. This is often a great way to get started and to share the ideas throughout your organization.

Training

The Lean approach is quite different from business as usual and often requires reinforcement throughout your organization structure. If you're committing to agile product and marketing practices it can make sense to provide in-depth training to key members of your team.
